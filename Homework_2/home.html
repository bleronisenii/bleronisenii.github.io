- La Masia, FC Barcelona's renowned youth academy, was founded in 1979 to develop local talent and embody the club's distinctive style. Under Johan Cruyff’s influence in the 1980s, the academy embraced a philosophy focused on technical skill and quick, possession-based play. La Masia’s reputation peaked in the 2000s with players like Xavi, Iniesta, and Messi, forming the backbone of a historic team led by Pep Guardiola. Today, La Masia continues to adapt and produce promising talents like Ansu Fati,Gavi and Lamine Yamal, remaining central to Barcelona’s identity and footballing tradition.

History

- La Masia was originally built as a traditional Catalan farmhouse in 1702. Known as Masia de Can Planes, it served as a rural farmhouse near Barcelona. By the time FC Barcelona acquired it, the building had aged significantly but retained its architectural charm and historical significance.

- In 1979, FC Barcelona converted La Masia into a residence for young players, making it the first official soccer academy of its kind in Spain. Over the following decades, it became synonymous with Barcelona's unique style of play and talent development philosophy. The farmhouse continued to serve as the academy's main facility until 2011, when a modern training center was built at Ciutat Esportiva Joan Gamper to accommodate the academy’s growth.
